# Trust and safety

Raydium is fully permissionless. That gives you flexibility, but it also means you need to verify what you are interacting with before you connect a wallet or sign a transaction.

## Verify the URL

Always check that you are on [Raydium.io](https://Raydium.io/) before connecting your wallet. This is Raydium's only domain. Variations you see on social media, in video descriptions, or in NFTs are scams.

## Verify transactions

Always double-check what you are signing in your wallet, even on legitimate sites.

## Do your own research

- **Check the token address** - Tickers and logos can be set by anyone. The mint address is the true identity of a token. Copy and paste contract addresses instead of typing tickers.
- **Read warnings** - Slippage alerts, unknown token prompts, and confirmation dialogs exist for a reason.
- **Use official channels** - Ask questions in official support channels only if you are unsure about something.

## Read the disclaimer

Before using Raydium, make sure you have read and understood the [disclaimer](https://Raydium.io/docs/disclaimer/).
